Sprout

2022

Prototyping a cozy, playful, and communal computing medium.

Weiwei Xu, May-Li Khoe, Max Bittker, Julius Tarng, Grant Custer, Steven Kao, Alexander Wang, Anya Likhitha, Aosheng Ran

We aimed to build a collaborative medium that evolves beyond personal computing paradigms—like syncing interaction states across all building blocks, so that people can share one slate of attention with each other.

The space is permissive-by-default to foster a sense of trust and intimacy, just like a studio where makers sit together with no barriers between; cursors can be a rich presence that indicates people's location and focus.

By centering the focal point from viewports to people's cursors, we can contextualize cursor movement as a digital body language. It helps us build shared understanding through unspoken context, i.e. how close people are to the exit.

Our social presence is more nuanced than online/offline. Our spaces should retain such nuance as we transition between the gradient of presence: we can reach into our backpack to look for things during a conversation without leaving the space.
